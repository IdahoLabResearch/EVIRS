Carefully consider stranded energy in electric vehicle batteries
  • The energy remaining in a damaged high-voltage lithium-ion battery, known as stranded energy, poses a risk of electric shock and creates the potential for thermal runaway.
  • Thermal runaway may cause multiple battery reignitions and fire after initial fire suppression.
  • Storing an electric vehicle with a damaged high-voltage lithium-ion battery inside the clear area (a recommended 50-foot-radius) may be infeasible at tow or storage yards.
